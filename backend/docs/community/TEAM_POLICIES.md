# Chioma Backend Team Policies and Guidelines

This document serves as the operational manual for the backend team, detailing working protocols, core processes, and team expectations.

## 1. Working Hours

*   **Flexibility**: Team members work across multiple time zones. We operate on a flexible working schedule that accommodates individual needs.
*   **Core Hours**: We maintain "Core Hours" (13:00 UTC - 17:00 UTC) where team members are expected to be available for synchronous communication and meetings.
*   **Responsiveness**: Outside core hours, communication is asynchronous. Response times are not expected to be immediate.

## 2. Communication Protocols

*   **Asynchronous First**: Prefer written, asynchronous communication (Slack/Discord channels, GitHub Discussions or PR comments) to respect others' flow states.
*   **Urgent Matters**: For major, production-impacting incidents, use PagerDuty or the dedicated #emergencies channel.
*   **Status Updates**: Keep your status updated (e.g., OOO, In a Meeting) to set proper expectations.

## 3. Meeting Standards

*   **Agendas**: Every meeting must have a clear agenda and purpose attached to the invite.
*   **Documentation**: Meeting notes, decisions, and action items must be recorded and shared with the team.
*   **Opt-In**: If a meeting does not directly require your input, it is optional. You may decline and catch up via notes.

## 4. Decision Making

*   **RFCs (Request for Comments)**: Any significant architectural or process change must go through a written RFC proposal and discussion period.
*   **Consensus**: We aim for consensus but prioritize progress. If consensus cannot be reached, the Team Lead or Architecture Lead makes the final call.
*   **Documentation**: All major decisions must be logged in an Architecture Decision Record (ADR).

## 5. Escalation Procedures

*   **Technical Blockers**: If blocked for more than 4 hours, escalate by mentioning the Team Lead or dropping a note in the dev channel.
*   **Non-Technical Issues**: Escalate interpersonal or project management issues directly to the Team Lead via a private 1-on-1.

## 6. Time Off

*   **Policy**: We encourage taking regular time off to prevent burnout.
*   **Notice**: Notify the team at least two weeks in advance for planned PTO of 3 days or more.
*   **Coverage**: Before leaving, ensure your responsibilities are covered, active PRs are handed off, and your calendar is blocked.

## 7. Remote Work

*   **Equipment**: We provide allowances for setting up a productive, ergonomic home office.
*   **Security**: Ensure local development environments and company data adhere to our security guidelines. Use standard VPNs and password managers.

## 8. On-Call Procedures

*   **Rotation**: Core backend developers participate in an on-call rotation to monitor production systems.
*   **Compensation/Time in Lieu**: Time spent responding to incidents outside standard hours is compensated appropriately (time off in lieu).
*   **Post-Mortems**: Every significant incident requires a blameless post-mortem document.

## 9. Feedback

*   **Continuous Feedback**: We value regular, continuous feedback rather than relying solely on annual reviews.
*   **1-on-1s**: Regular 1-on-1s are held between developers and the Team Lead for professional development and direct feedback.

## 10. Professional Development

*   **Growth**: Time is allocated monthly for learning new technologies or working on Chioma-related side projects.
*   **Conferences/Courses**: Team members can request budgets for conferences, courses, and books that benefit their role.
